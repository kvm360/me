How It Works

A simple 4-step testing procedure

01

Transfer Blood Sample

Using the supplied plastic pipette, transfer approximately 1 mL of whole blood onto the testing card.

02

Add Buffer Solution

Add one drop of the supplied buffer solution (approximately 45 μL) into each well of the testing card.

03

Mix with Wooden Stick

Mix each well for about 15 seconds using a different stick per well to prevent cross-contamination.

04

Read Results

Read results within 2 minutes. Do not read after 5 minutes, as results may become inaccurate.

Why Blood Typing Matters

Confirming compatibility saves lives

Avoid Transfusion Reactions

Dogs have naturally occurring blood antigens. Mismatched blood can cause hemolysis, shock or fatal complications.

Scientifically Validated

Kai 1 and Kai 2 blood types were identified through advanced methods and published in the peer-reviewed journal PLOS ONE (2017).

Emergency Preparedness

Knowing blood type in advance avoids delays during emergencies, ensuring safe, compatible treatment when seconds matter.

Enhanced Safety with Kai Blood Typing

Kai 1 and Kai 2 are newly discovered blood types that can explain hidden transfusion risks. Identifying these types before transfusion significantly reduces the chance of adverse reactions. When combined with the standard DEA1 test, Kai blood typing adds an extra layer of safety — giving every patient the best possible outcome.
